"Software that improves people's lives — that's a promise that only works if the software is reliable, secure, and trustworthy. One breach and you're not improving lives, you're compromising them."

**[WHY FINEST — 15s**
"Finest's mission resonates with me — building software that genuinely helps people. But that mission only holds if the software is built on a secure foundation. Your high conversion rate from intern to full-time tells me you invest in people and grow with them. That's the kind of environment where security culture takes root — not through policies, but through shared engineering values."

**[WHAT I BRING — 15s]**
"I bring an offensive security perspective combined with real production engineering. I've built concurrent systems in Golang, reverse-engineered complex JavaScript architectures, and developed production-grade APIs with async processing, Docker, and PostgreSQL. I don't just find bugs — I understand *why* systems fail and how to build them right from the start. My CTO experience at MatX.ee means I can bridge the gap between security concerns and product decisions."

**[CLOSING — 5s]**
"Finest builds software that matters. I want to make sure that software is built to last — secure, reliable, and worthy of the trust people place in it."

## Delivery Notes

- **Lead with the mission** — Finest's brand is about improving lives, connect security to that
- **Emphasize** "shared engineering values" — aligns with their culture-first approach
- **On high conversion rate** — shows you see this as a long-term opportunity, not just an internship
- **CTO bridge** — unique angle that most candidates won't have
- **Final sentence** — "worthy of the trust" connects security to their core value proposition

## Key Topics to Have Ready

- Security culture in small, mission-driven teams
- How security integrates with product development workflows
- Remote/hybrid security practices (Fahle office + remote flexibility)
- Software reliability and uptime as a security concern
- Long-term career growth from intern to security leadership
